Drag the white dot z. The path connects the partial sums S₀ = 1, S₁ = 1 + z, S₂ = 1 + z + z², … — each new segment is the next term zⁿ, so the path rotates by arg z and scales by |z| at every step. Inside the dashed unit circle the spiral closes in on 1/(1−z) (the × mark); outside, it flies apart; right on the circle it circles forever without settling. The amber ε-disk is the "challenge" of the ε–N definition of convergence: N(ε) is the cutoff past which every partial sum stays inside the disk — shrink ε and watch the required N grow. Points that are inside for good turn green.
