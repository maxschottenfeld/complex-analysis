The Complex Logarithm & Its Branch Cut

Every nonzero z = reiθ has infinitely many logarithms: log z = ln|z| + i(θ + 2πk),  k ∈ ℤ. The principal branch picks k = 0 with Arg z ∈ (−π, π], giving Log z = ln|z| + i·Arg z.

To make Log z single-valued we must cut the plane along the negative real axis. Crossing that cut, Arg z jumps from +π to −π — that jump is the branch cut. This page makes that visible from four angles.

Panel 1 Domain coloring of Log z

Each point of the plane is colored by its value of Log z: hue = Arg z (the imaginary part) and brightness = ln|z| (the real part — dark near 0, bright far out). Watch the negative real axis: the hue snaps from one color straight to its opposite. That hard seam is the discontinuity of Log z.

Arg ≈ 0 (positive real) Arg ≈ +π/2 (up) Arg ≈ −π/2 (down) ◖ seam on negative-x axis = branch cut

○ at the origin: Log 0 is undefined — a logarithmic singularity. The same point z is also live in Panels 2 & 3.

Panel 3 Walk around the origin — where continuity breaks

A point travels counterclockwise on a circle of radius r. On the right, Arg z is plotted against the angle traveled. It climbs smoothly from −π to +π — then, the instant the point crosses the negative real axis, it jumps straight back to −π. The function can't stay continuous there. That forced jump is exactly why a branch cut is needed.

⚠ Arg z just jumped +π → −π — this is the branch cut!

Panel 4 The Riemann surface — the log's “true” domain

The jump in Panel 3 is an artifact of forcing one value per point. The honest picture is a helicoid: height = Im(log z) = the argument, which keeps increasing by 2π each lap around the origin instead of resetting. Each full turn is a different sheet (a different k). The log is perfectly continuous up here — drag to rotate. The green band is the principal sheet (k = 0); the branch cut is just the edge where we slice one sheet off to flatten it back into Panel 1.

Drag horizontally to spin · the vertical seams between colors are the θ = ±π edges where consecutive sheets join.
