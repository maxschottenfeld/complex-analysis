Drag the white dot z₀ and the two vector tips (coral / cyan) on the left. The right panel shows each vector multiplied by the derivative f′(z₀) as a complex number — a rotation by arg(f′) and a scaling by |f′|. Because the same rotation hits both vectors, the angle between them is unchanged: that's conformality. For z̄ the local map is a reflection (Jacobian [[1,0],[0,−1]]), which flips orientation and reverses the angle. Where f′(z₀)=0 (origin for z², z³) the map collapses and angles are destroyed.
