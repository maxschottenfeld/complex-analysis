Why deformation invariance holds

If f is holomorphic on the region between two closed contours γ1, γ2 (no singularities in between), then ∮γ1f dz = ∮γ2f dz.
Reason: apply Cauchy–Goursat to the boundary of the in-between region — γ1 together with −γ2 bounds a region where f is holomorphic, so the integral over that whole boundary is 0.
The integral only changes when the contour crosses the singularity — each crossing changes the winding number by ±1, and the value by ±2πi.
